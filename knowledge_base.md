## General · Expense Ratio (What Is TER)

**Fact:** Total Expense Ratio (TER) represents all costs for running and managing a mutual fund scheme, expressed as a percentage of the scheme's average daily NAV. TER includes investment management fees, registrar fees, custodian fees, audit fees, and sales & marketing expenses. SEBI prescribes maximum TER limits — for the first ₹500 crores of AUM in an equity scheme, the maximum is 2.25%.

## General · Exit Load (How It Works)

**Fact:** The repurchase/redemption price includes the deduction of exit load (if applicable) from the NAV. Redemption price is calculated as: Applicable NAV × (1 − Exit Load). Entry loads have been abolished by SEBI since 2009. In open-ended schemes, repurchase price shall not be lower than 95% of NAV.

## General · Riskometer (SEBI Mandate)

**Fact:** SEBI mandates all mutual fund schemes to display a Risk-o-meter with **6 levels: Low, Low-to-Moderate, Moderate, Moderately High, High, and Very High**, updated monthly based on portfolio composition.
